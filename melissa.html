Founding Partner

Melissa Rueschhoff

Trial attorney, prosecutor, professor, advocate, and leader dedicated to protecting vulnerable populations, empowering entrepreneurs, and pursuing justice through disciplined legal advocacy.

Biography

A career built on advocacy.

Melissa has been licensed to practice law in Colorado, Texas, Arizona, Alabama, and Hawaii, as well as six federal courts. Her experience spans litigation, prosecution, legislative advocacy, higher education, public policy, and complex legal counseling.

Melissa's legal career has been defined by a commitment to justice, public service, and advocacy. Early in her practice, she focused extensively on criminal prosecution, serving in Special Victims Units and working to combat child sex trafficking both domestically and internationally.

In Hawaii, she served as an Analyst and Special Prosecutor for the Hawaii Internet Crimes Against Children Unit within the Hawaii Attorney General's Office. She also served as a Legislative Attorney for the Judiciary Chair at the Hawaii State Legislature, helping shape policy affecting families, children, and communities.

Beyond courtroom advocacy, Melissa served as a law professor at the University of Arizona Rogers College of Law, Faulkner University's Jones School of Law, and the University of Hawaii Richardson School of Law, helping educate future attorneys and legal leaders.

Today, as a Founding Partner at Signature Law Partners, PLLC, she continues to advocate for women and children, assist entrepreneurs in building successful businesses, and provide determined representation in complex legal matters.

Community Impact

Service beyond the courtroom.

Melissa has served on Hawaii's Joint Venture Education Forum, Ho'Ola Na Pua's Executive Board of Directors, the CGO Foundation, and the Common Sense Media Advisory Board.

As a USAF senior leader spouse, she is also a sought-after speaker at command conferences, women's leadership symposiums, and national and international events focused on service, leadership, and advocacy.
